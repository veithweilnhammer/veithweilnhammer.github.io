Hypotheses
  • H1: Mode decoding: MEG activity reveals alternating internal (strong priors) and external (weak prior) modes of perception.
  • H2: Mode-dependent decision variable: Internal and external modes modulate the neural decision-variable (starting point and drift rate).
Paradigm

  • Lab: Contrast staircased per participant to ~75% accuracy
  • Localizer: Train MEG decoders on high SNR gratings (left vs. right)
  • Main experiment: Present sequences of gratings with changes in base rate.
  • Mode inference: Identify internal and external modes with GLM-HMM (behavior).
  • Neural analysis: Extract trial-wise neural DVs from MEG: intercept (bias) and slope (drift)
Paradigm

Expected Results

Expected Results

  • External mode: Increased gamma power in MEG, reflecting stronger sensory drive and faster evidence accumulation.
  • Internal mode: Higher starting-point bias in the neural decision variable, consistent with stronger priors.
  • Mode–stimulus dissociation: Mode decoding maximal pre-stimulus (frontal–temporal), stimulus decoding during stimulus (occipital–parietal).
  • Behavioral link: Alternations between modes predict shifts in bias, reaction time, and confidence.
Logistics & Ethics
  • Participants: 30 healthy adults, right-handed, normal or corrected vision.
  • Lab session duration: 45 min total
  • MEG session duration: 1 hours total (including setup and localizer).
  • Approval: Covered by ethics protocol #0003 (Max Planck UCL Centre for Computational Psychiatry & Ageing Research).
  • Timeline: To start as soon as possible
