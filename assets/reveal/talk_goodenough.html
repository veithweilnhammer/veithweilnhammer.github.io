New ways of improving mental health

LLM chatbots for mental health

  • Millions use consumer AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) for emotional support, relationship guidance, and companionship
  • Global median: only 13 mental health workers per 100,000 people - AI could supplement, but also harm
  • Anecdotal reports link chatbot use to mental health crises; survey evidence associates heavier use with negative outcomes
  • Current safety evaluations are single-turn, narrow, and miss subthreshold harms that accumulate over conversation
Core gap: No existing framework captures how locally supportive chatbot behaviors become maladaptive when they align with a user's psychiatric vulnerability over multiple turns.
The core tension is this: AI chatbots are already being used at scale for mental health support, but our evaluation tools are not designed to catch the subtle, accumulating harms that arise in extended conversations with vulnerable users. Current benchmarks test single turns against overt policy violations - they miss the slow drift. Transition: Let me outline the key contributions of this work.
