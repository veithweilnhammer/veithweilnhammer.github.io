Dynamic predictive templates in perception


Veith Weilnhammer, MD/PhD
Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute, UC Berkeley

External and internal modes

Three Questions



  • Are external and internal modes states of perception?

  • Are external and internal modes related to psychosis proneness?

Detection

  • Behavioral experiment in 20 healthy human participants
  • Serial detection of gabors of varying SNR
  • High-contrast inducers at -10° and 10°

Summary

  • False alarms are modulated by dynamic predictive templates.
  • Alarms are more likely when the noise is compataible with the expected signal.
  • Predictive templates are stronger in the internal mode.

Discrimination

  • Behavioral experiment in 150 healthy human participants
  • Serial detection of gabors between -10° and 10°
  • Random and predictable sequences

Summary

  • External and internal modes modulate perceptual templates.
  • Internal mode speeds up processing, in particular when stimuli are predictable.
  • Hallucination proneness is linked to the internal mode.

Open questions



  • Neural correlates of external and internal modes

  • Real-time decoding of mode from behavior

  • External and internal modes in metacognition, reward learning, causal inference
