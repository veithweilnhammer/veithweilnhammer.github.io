# Review — opus

**Thesis (one sentence):** Psychiatry's leading research programs no longer treat DSM
categories as natural kinds but as convenient administrative fictions, so the manual
that governs the clinic no longer matches how the field's own science describes mental
illness. The draft develops exactly this one argument start to finish — no branching,
no second thesis.

Overall this is strong: well-structured, one self-contained argument, brief fully
covered, facts sound. The issues are almost all voice/style, concentrated in a handful
of antithesis and "trade" constructions the style guide explicitly bans. Fix those and
it ships.

## 1. Style-guide violations (priority order)

**a. Parallel "opposite trade" construction — the clearest hard-ban hit (para 12).**
> "Treating a category as a discovered disease makes crossing its threshold feel like
> uncovering something that was already fully present; treating it as a practical
> construct keeps the threshold visible as a choice to be used with care."

This is the banned `Treating it as X does A; treating it as Y does B` parallelism (cf.
"Behaviorism kept the movement… Psychoanalysis made the opposite trade"). Split into two
plain, separate statements. Fix:
> "If the category is taken as a discovered disease, crossing its threshold looks like
> detecting something that was already there. Seen as a practical construct, the
> threshold stays visible as a decision that should be applied carefully."
(Better, but still leans parallel — consider dropping the first clause entirely and just
stating the second.)

**b. Punchy setup sentence + "X rather than Y" (para 10).**
> "This is where 'convenient fiction' becomes an accurate description rather than an
> insult."
Announcement sentence that also uses `X rather than Y`. Fix: state it directly —
> "'Convenient fiction' is a precise description of what the categories have become."

**c. "X rather than Y" metaphor pairs (several).** Each is mild on its own; together they
are a tic. Rewrite the ones carrying a metaphor:
- Para 4: "the categories are boundaries drawn through continuous territory **rather than
  walls around isolated conditions**." → "…the categories look like lines drawn across
  continuous territory; the overlap is what you would expect if the conditions are not
  separate."
- Para 9: "Everything in it is a matter of degree along a continuum **rather than a box a
  person is inside or outside.**" → "Everything in it is a matter of degree: a position on
  a continuum, not membership in a box." (or just: "…is measured as a position on a
  continuum.")
- Para 8: "the mechanisms of mental illness follow the brain's functional architecture
  **rather than the chapter headings of a manual**" → keep the point, drop the flourish:
  "…follow the brain's functional systems, which need not line up with the manual's
  categories."

Note: "RDoC… is a research framework, not a clinical tool" (para 8) reads as `not X but Y`
but it is Insel's own attributed wording — leave it.

**d. Understatement / throat-clearing opener (para 11).**
> "The honest complication is that the clinic cannot simply switch."
Cousin of the banned "The tension is not academic." Fix: "The clinic cannot simply
switch, for two reasons." (then give them).

**e. Enumerated short openers (paras 4–7): "The first is comorbidity." / "The second is
heterogeneity…" / "The third is reliability…" / "The fourth is the missing biology."**
Borderline, not a hard ban — the parallel signposting is clear and readable. But four
clipped `The Nth is X` openers in a row edges toward punchy cadence. Optional: fold at
least one or two into the following sentence (e.g., "Comorbidity is the first strain:
in the National Comorbidity Survey Replication…").

## 2. Factual grounding (independently spot-checked)

All key claims verify; nothing over-claimed. Confirmed via web search:
- Insel 2013 "Transforming Diagnosis" quotes ("reliability… lack of validity";
  "ischemic heart disease, lymphoma, or AIDS… not any objective laboratory measure";
  re-orienting away from DSM categories) — accurate.
- RDoC six domains, sensorimotor added 2019 — accurate.
- Fried & Nesse STAR*D: 1,030 profiles among 3,703, most common ~1.8% — accurate (and the
  Evidence flag correctly drops the mis-attributed "227" figure).
- 636,120 PTSD combinations (Galatzer-Levy & Bryant), NCS-R 45% comorbidity, DSM-5 field-
  trial kappas (MDD ~0.28, GAD ~0.20), Kraepelin/dementia praecox, DSM-III 1980/Spitzer —
  all consistent with the Evidence and standard sources.
- Insel's "did not measurably reduce suicide or hospitalization" is correctly presented as
  a paraphrase, not a quote (matches the Evidence flag). Leave hedged as-is.

One small caution: the kappa figures are field-trial values — the draft properly frames
them ("When the DSM-5 was field-tested") and does not present 0.28 as depression's
reliability everywhere. Good; keep that framing intact through any edit.

## 3. Structure & clarity

- Opens on a concrete real event (the 2013 announcement) with the claim up front. Good.
- A non-specialist can follow it: natural kind, validity vs. utility, categorical vs.
  dimensional, and "convenient fiction" are each defined in plain language before use.
- Ordering is sound: strain in the data → RDoC → HiTOP → what "fiction" means → caveats →
  close. No redundancy or out-of-order material.
- Minor: para 2 "That announcement carried weight because of who made it and what he
  controlled." is slightly meta/telling; consider merging its substance into the following
  sentence so the paragraph shows the weight rather than asserting it. Low priority.

## 4. One self-contained argument

Yes. Every paragraph serves the single thesis; the overdiagnosis paragraph (12) stays a
supporting consequence and does not swell into a separate medicalizing-suffering thesis
(correctly respecting the brief's "do NOT re-tell"). No series framing, no next/prev, no
CTA. Stands alone.

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone

All "must cover" items present: what the DSM is/does (para 3); natural-kinds strain via
comorbidity, heterogeneity, reliability, missing biology (4–7); RDoC (8); HiTOP + p factor
(9); meaning of "convenient fiction" (10); stakes + honest caveat + overdiagnosis (11–12).
Correctly avoids the value-laden-threshold, 1973 homosexuality removal, and Hacking
looping-effects material owned by post 12. Length 1,960 words — inside the 1,600–2,000
target (near the ceiling, so prefer trims over additions).

## Top 3 priorities

1. **Rewrite the para 12 "treating it as X… treating it as Y…" parallel** — the one
   unambiguous hard-ban violation. Split into plain separate statements.
2. **Kill the punchy "…accurate description rather than an insult" (para 10)** and the
   handful of "X rather than Y" metaphor pairs (paras 4, 8, 9); state each positive claim
   directly.
3. **De-tic the openers**: soften "The honest complication is that…" (para 11) and fold
   one or two of the four `The Nth is…` sentence-openers (paras 4–7) into their following
   sentences for a more measured cadence.

**Verdict: ship-with-minor-edits** — factually sound and structurally complete; only a
few localized antithesis/"trade" constructions need flattening to match the style guide.
