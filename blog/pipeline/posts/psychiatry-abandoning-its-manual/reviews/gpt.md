## Style-guide violations

- “It is a categorical system.” This is a short announcement sentence. Fold it into the next sentence: “The DSM is categorical: for each disorder…”
- “Several features of the data sit awkwardly against that hope. The first is comorbidity.” This is scaffolded and punchy. Fix by stating the evidence directly: “High comorbidity is the first problem for that hope.”
- “The second is heterogeneity within a single category.” Same issue. Fix: “Within-category heterogeneity creates the same problem inside a diagnosis.”
- “The third is reliability under closer scrutiny.” Same issue. Fix: “Field trials also showed uneven reliability for some common diagnoses.”
- “The fourth is the missing biology.” Same issue and slightly absolute. Fix: “The biological evidence has not produced individual-level tests that map cleanly onto DSM labels.”
- “rather than walls around isolated conditions,” “the sharp walls between DSM categories,” and “the chapter headings of a manual” are useful metaphors but accumulate. Keep one at most; prefer “thresholds on continuous variation.”
- “the jobs labels do well” and “the structure of the thing itself” are vague/essayistic. Fix with concrete nouns: “communication, billing, and treatment access” / “the mechanisms that produce symptoms.”
