# Review — opus

**Thesis (one sentence):** Loneliness is an evolved alarm that pushes people
toward reconnection, and AI companions that mute the feeling without restoring
real connection risk disabling that function and leaving the person isolated.
The draft develops exactly this one argument start to finish — no second thesis,
no branching. Coverage of the brief's must-cover list is complete.

## Priority fixes (highest impact first)

1. **Closing wordplay is a poetic coda — the guide's clearest ending ban.**
   > "Feeling less lonely is worth measuring, and it should be measured alongside
   > whether the person ends up less alone."
   The "less lonely / less alone" chime is a rhetorical flourish carrying the
   ending. Land the substantive point flatly instead, e.g.: "The feeling of being
   less lonely is worth measuring, but it should be measured alongside whether the
   person actually spends more time with other people." Also reword the preceding
   image "once the screen is dark" → "after the conversation ends."

2. **Antithesis / "opposite-trade" parallelism — banned rhythm.**
   > "The apps are effective at the thing they measure, the momentary feeling, and
   > silent on the thing the feeling exists to report, the person's actual social
   > connection."
   This is the "effective at X … silent on Y" trade construction the guide flags
   as clever-but-hollow. Split into two plain sentences: "The apps reliably reduce
   the momentary feeling of loneliness. They do nothing about the person's actual
   social connection, which is the state that feeling exists to report on."

3. **Second trade-parallel + flourish in the bridge/substitute paragraph.**
   > "A system designed around reconnection would notice withdrawal and treat
   > successful offline connection as the goal, while a system designed around
   > engagement would count return visits and session length as success, and the
   > two could feel identical in a single conversation while diverging over months."
   The paired "designed around reconnection … designed around engagement" plus the
   "feel identical … while diverging over months" tail is exactly the parallel
   rhythm to avoid. Keep the substance (it's a real point) but break the symmetry
   and drop the flourish, e.g. state each design's success metric in a separate
   sentence and end on the plain observation that the difference only shows up over
   months of use.

## Minor style flags

4. **Throat-clearing thesis opener.**
   > "The question worth asking is whether a system that reduces the feeling of
   > loneliness also restores the social connection that loneliness normally
   > signals."
   Borderline "here's the question" framing. Tighter: "Whether a system that
   reduces the feeling of loneliness also restores the connection it signals is
   the question this raises."

5. **Mild "X rather than Y" antithesis.**
   > "…where the whole question turns, and it is empirical rather than
   > philosophical."
   Say it positively: "…and it is an empirical question — it depends on what the
   person actually does after using the app."

6. **Faintly evocative phrase** (acceptable, but on the edge):
   > "wake up with the same empty calendar and the same absence of anyone who would
   > notice if they disappeared."
   Concrete enough to keep; if trimming, cut "the same absence of anyone who would
   notice if they disappeared" to "and still knowing no one who would notice."
