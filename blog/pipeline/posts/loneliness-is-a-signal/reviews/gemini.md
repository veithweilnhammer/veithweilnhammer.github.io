## 2. Factual grounding
Grounding is solid. The Surgeon General's statistics (15 cigarettes, 29/32/50% risk increases) align with the underlying Holt-Lunstad meta-analysis and HHS advisory. The studies (De Freitas et al., 2025; Maples et al., 2024) are appropriately summarized and hedged. No factual errors or overclaims found.

## 3. Structure & clarity
The structure is strong. It leads with a concrete problem and builds the argument logically in a way a non-specialist can easily follow.

## 4. One self-contained argument
The post strictly adheres to its single thesis: Loneliness is an evolved alarm signal that pushes people toward reconnection, and AI companions that soothe the feeling without restoring connection risk disabling that function.

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
The draft covers all "must cover" items perfectly and avoids all "do NOT re-tell" items. It reads entirely as a standalone post without any series framing or CTAs.

## 6. Top 3 priorities
1. Eradicate the "not X, but Y" and parallel "trade" constructions (e.g., "The apps are effective at the thing they measure... and silent on the thing the feeling exists to report", "The signal tracks perceived connection, not a simple count").
2. Remove punchy setup sentences and throat-clearing (e.g., "In this account the discomfort is what makes the system work", "The point is narrower, and it is about what to watch").
3. Strip pseudo-poetic scene-setting and rhetorical crescendos, particularly in the closing paragraph ("once the screen is dark", "Feeling less lonely... less alone") and the chatbot user hypothetical ("same empty calendar").
