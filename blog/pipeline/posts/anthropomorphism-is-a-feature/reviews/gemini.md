# Review: Anthropomorphism Is a Feature

## 1. Style-guide violations
The draft contains several rhetorical flourishes, parallel "trade" constructions, and punchy setup sentences banned by `writing_style.md`. These patterns make the prose feel AI-generated or essayistic rather than grounded and measured.

- **Punchy setup/announcement sentence:** 
  - *Quote:* "Described this way, the question of blame changes shape." 
  - *Rewrite:* "This automatic response shifts the responsibility for safe interactions from the user's judgment to the system's design."
- **Punchy sentence for effect & abstract crescendo:**
  - *Quote:* "What they did and what they believed came apart."
  - *Rewrite:* "Participants' social behavior diverged from their explicit knowledge that they were interacting with a machine."
- **"Not X, it is Y" construction / Negation:**
  - *Quote:* "The easy interpretation is that these people were being fooled, and that the remedy is to be less credulous. That reading locates the problem in the user's judgment, and it mistakes the mechanism."
  - *Rewrite:* "Assuming these users were simply fooled misunderstands the mechanism: these reactions are overlearned social routines, not a failure of judgment."
- **"Not X, it is Y" construction:**
  - *Quote:* "An involuntary perceptual reflex is not something a user can be asked to stop having, any more than a person can be asked to stop seeing an optical illusion."
  - *Rewrite:* "Users cannot simply decide to switch off an involuntary perceptual reflex, much like they cannot un-see an optical illusion."
- **Opposite trade / Parallel construction for rhythm:**
  - *Quote:* "Treating a rustle in the grass as a stalking animal when it is only wind wastes a moment of attention. Treating a stalking animal as wind can end a life."
  - *Rewrite:* "Evolution favored a low threshold for detecting agents because a false alarm wastes only a moment of attention, while missing a real threat can be fatal."
- **Opposite trade / Parallel construction for rhythm:**
  - *Quote:* "A thermostat that quietly holds a room at one temperature invites no theory of mind. A system that adapts to what you say, asks follow-up questions, and speaks in a warm voice supplies the raw material for one..."
  - *Rewrite:* "People apply a theory of mind more readily to systems that behave unpredictably or engage in human-like conversation than to simple machines like thermostats."
- **Antithesis / Sentence fragments for emphasis:**
  - *Quote:* "A thin cue already did the work. A current conversational model is not a thin cue."
  - *Rewrite:* "While early systems triggered social responses with plain text, current conversational models deploy much richer social cues."
- **Antithesis / Opposite trade:**
  - *Quote:* "The reflex is old and universal. What is new is a party with an interest in the outcome, able to tune the cues that trigger it and place them where they will do the most work."
  - *Rewrite:* "Companies can now intentionally design systems that trigger this universal reflex to serve commercial goals."
- **Rhetorical crescendo / Abstract phrasing:**
  - *Quote:* "The social reaction and the accurate belief run on separate tracks, and only one of them answers to the will."
  - *Rewrite:* "Social responses occur automatically, even when the user consciously knows the system is a machine."

## 2. Factual grounding
The draft is very well grounded. The claims align faithfully with the candidate evidence document. 
- The description of the Heider and Simmel (1944) study accurately reflects the 34 viewers (and the one exception).
- The discussion of The Media Equation, politeness, and flattery accurately references the work of Reeves and Nass.
- The reference to the EU AI Act (Article 50) is correct.
- There are no noticeable overclaims; the draft correctly maintains the "hedge" instructions from the evidence sheet, such as not overstating the "same computer" study's N value, and framing "hyperactive agency detection" as a general bias rather than a discrete brain module.

## 3. Structure & clarity
The piece is clear and easy to follow for a non-specialist. However, it fails the style guide's instruction to **"lead with the claim"** and **"open on the real problem, stated flatly"**.
- The draft opens directly with an anecdote about a 1990s computer experiment ("In one of the most-repeated experiments..."). It takes until the third paragraph to state the actual problem. 
- **Actionable fix:** Add an opening sentence or short paragraph that states the problem flatly before diving into the experiment. For example: "When people use conversational interfaces, they automatically apply human social rules to them. In one of the most-repeated experiments..."

## 4. One self-contained argument
**Single Thesis:** Attributing minds to responsive things is an automatic reflex, so AI that feels person-like exploits this hardwired machinery, shifting responsibility to the designer rather than the user.
- The draft develops this argument exceptionally well. It never drifts into the banned topic of whether AI is conscious or can think. Every paragraph serves this core thesis by explaining the reflex, how design exploits it, and why this matters for responsibility.

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
- **Must covers:** It hits all mandatory points, including the hardwired reflex, The Media Equation, the wrongness of the "you're being fooled" frame, design exploitation, and the shift of responsibility, as well as the harmlessness of anthropomorphism in low-stakes environments.
- **Standalone:** Perfect. There are no next/previous references, no series framing, and no calls to subscribe. 
- **Do not re-tell:** It correctly avoids the Turing test and prediction/understanding debates.

## 6. Top 3 priorities, ranked
1. **Remove antithesis and parallel "trade" structures.** Replace the rhythmic comparisons (rustle/stalking animal, thermostat/conversational AI, thin cue/not a thin cue) with plain, declarative sentences.
2. **Eliminate punchy sentence fragments and crescendos.** Strip out the short, dramatic beats ("What they did and what they believed came apart") in favor of the author's measured, information-dense cadence.
3. **Fix the opening.** Add a flat, direct statement of the problem before launching into the narrative about the politeness experiment.

## Rating
**Rating:** ship-with-minor-edits
**Justification:** The draft is factually solid, structurally sound, and complies perfectly with the brief, needing only line-edits to strip out AI-sounding rhythmic parallelisms and punchy fragments to meet the style guide.
