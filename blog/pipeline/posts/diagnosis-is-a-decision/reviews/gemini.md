# Review

## 1. Style-guide violations
The draft violates several hard bans, particularly around antithesis, punchy setup sentences, rhetorical crescendos, and understatement openers.

**Antithesis ("not X, but Y") / "opposite trade" constructions:**
*   *Draft:* "Which error you would rather make... is a question about what a health system owes people and what it can afford, not one the symptom scores can answer by themselves."
    *Rewrite:* "Symptom scores cannot answer whether to over-treat the mild or overlook the serious; that choice depends on what a health system owes people and can afford."
*   *Draft:* "This is the point at which automated screening stops being a footnote and becomes the main event."
    *Rewrite:* Delete entirely. Start the paragraph directly with the claim: "Automated screening tools inherit a chosen cut-point..."
*   *Draft:* "...its users are strongly inclined to treat a number from a machine as a discovery rather than a policy."
    *Rewrite:* "...its users are strongly inclined to treat a machine's output as an objective discovery."
*   *Draft:* "A human clinician can treat a threshold as a guide rather than a verdict..."
    *Rewrite:* "A human clinician can treat a threshold as a guide, noticing a recent job loss..."
*   *Draft:* "A threshold remembered as a decision can be revisited... A threshold mistaken for a discovery hardens into a supposed fact..."
    *Rewrite:* "Thresholds can be revisited when evidence or values change, provided they are remembered as decisions. Mistaking them for natural discoveries hardens them into supposed facts about people that no vote seems entitled to touch."
*   *Draft:* "The 1973 vote is worth remembering not as an embarrassment but as an unusually honest moment..."
    *Rewrite:* "The 1973 vote remains an unusually honest moment when a profession acknowledged..."

**Punchy setup sentences and fragments:**
*   *Draft:* "Once a decision like that is made, it does not sit quietly as a description."
    *Rewrite:* Delete and integrate into the next sentence: "Classifying people changes them in a way that classifying rocks does not, as the philosopher Ian Hacking argued."
*   *Draft:* "This feedback is not always harmful."
    *Rewrite:* "A diagnosis can give a person language for a pattern..."
*   *Draft:* "The category changed when the threshold changed."
    *Rewrite:* Delete (it is a dramatic one-sentence summary that breaks cadence).

**Rhetorical crescendos / flourishes:**
*   *Draft:* "A diagnosis is a description that acts on the thing it describes." (End of paragraph 7)
    *Rewrite:* Delete. Let the Hacking quotation make the point on its own.
*   *Draft:* "The line was drawn by a decision, and the drawing of it reaches back and reshapes the distribution it was meant to divide." (End of paragraph 8)
    *Rewrite:* Delete.

**Understatement openers:**
*   *Draft:* "None of this is an argument against drawing lines."
    *Rewrite:* "Health systems with finite clinicians must use thresholds to decide who receives care."

**Evocative image-lists and piled-up lists:**
*   *Draft:* "...how someone types, sleeps, moves, or phrases their messages..."
    *Rewrite:* "...how someone types or sleeps..." (Pick two and move on).
*   *Draft:* "A school, an insurer, a hiring platform, a primary-care clinic, and a crisis line do not face the same costs..."

## 2. Factual grounding
*   *Draft:* "The boundary of one illness was redrawn, and it was redrawn by a show of hands."
    The 1974 vote (5,854 to 3,810) was a mailed ballot referendum sent to over 10,000 APA members, not a literal "show of hands." This is a rhetorical stretch that introduces a factual error. Rewrite to state plainly that it was ratified by a vote.
*   The remainder of the factual claims (PHQ-9 cutoffs, DSM depression criteria, Spitzer's definitional argument, Hacking's looping effects) match the evidence file perfectly.

## 3. Structure & clarity
The draft flows very well. It effectively opens with the concrete 1973 homosexuality case, establishes the intuition of "discovery", brings in the dimensional nature of symptoms, introduces looping effects, and applies the entire framework to automated AI screening. The logic is straightforward and highly accessible to a non-specialist.

## 4. One self-contained argument
**Thesis:** The boundary of psychiatric illness is a socially chosen threshold on a continuous spectrum rather than a natural discovery; these labels loop back to change people, and AI screening systems inherit and enforce these chosen thresholds at scale.

The post develops this single argument systematically from start to finish. It successfully uses concrete anchors (the APA vote, PHQ-9 scoring, multiple personalities) without drifting into loosely related topics. Every paragraph serves the core thesis. 

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
The draft satisfies the "must cover" items from the brief, and operates perfectly as a standalone essay. It contains no next/previous links, subscribe CTAs, or series framing. 

Most importantly, it complies with the "Do NOT re-tell" constraint regarding the sibling post (`psychiatry-abandoning-its-manual`). While it uses the DSM's 5-out-of-9 symptoms requirement for major depression to illustrate cutoffs on a continuum, it refrains from repeating the broader "categories aren't natural kinds" or RDoC/HiTOP arguments that belong to the sibling post.

## 6. Top 3 priorities
1. **Purge the "not X, but Y" and antithesis constructions**, especially the final paragraph's "not as an embarrassment but as an unusually honest moment" and the "discovery rather than a policy" / "guide rather than a verdict" formulations.
2. **Flatten the punchy setup sentences and rhetorical sign-offs** (e.g., "This is the point at which automated screening stops being a footnote and becomes the main event", "A diagnosis is a description that acts on the thing it describes") to restore the measured, information-dense cadence of the author's manuscripts.
3. **Fix the "show of hands" factual error** regarding the 1974 mail-in ballot.
