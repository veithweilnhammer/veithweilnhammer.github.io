# Brief: If a Chatbot Prevents One Suicide and Causes Another, How Do We Count It?

## Slug

## Working title
If a Chatbot Prevents One Suicide and Causes Another, How Do We Count It?

## One-line angle (also the Substack subtitle / SEO hook)
Deployed to millions, a mental-health chatbot will both prevent and cause harm, and scale forces us to do explicitly the moral arithmetic medicine usually keeps implicit.

## Single argument (thesis)
At population scale a conversational AI will both prevent and cause serious harm, so evaluating it requires an explicit net-benefit calculation — the same moral arithmetic behind screening and public-health decisions — rather than judging it by individual tragedies alone.

## Concrete anchor (optional — a real example to ground the argument)
The Character.AI lawsuits (2024–2026) alleging a chatbot contributed to a teenager's suicide — a real, specific harm that any honest accounting has to weigh against the unseen cases where a chatbot may have helped.

## What this post must cover
- The framing problem: a single documented harm is vivid and a prevented harm is invisible, so public judgment fixes on the tragedy while ignoring the counterfactual.
- Why scale forces the arithmetic: at millions of users, both prevented and caused harms are near-certain, so "do no harm" as an absolute is not an available option — the real question is the net.
- The concrete anchor: Character.AI lawsuits (2024–2026) as a case where causation, foreseeability, and responsibility are contested in public.
- The established tools medicine already uses for exactly this trade-off: number-needed-to-treat, number-needed-to-harm, and the false-positive/false-negative trade-offs in screening.
- Why the arithmetic is genuinely hard: prevented harms are unmeasured, causation is contested, harms and benefits are not commensurable, and who bears each is not evenly distributed.
- What honest accounting demands: measurement and comparison groups, not just anecdote — and a willingness to state the trade-off out loud rather than pretend a safe option with zero harm exists.
