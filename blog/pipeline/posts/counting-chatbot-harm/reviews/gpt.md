1. **Fix hard-ban style patterns before anything else.** The draft has a strong argument, but several lines use banned antithesis, punchy setup, and rhetorical understatement. These need line edits, not a full conceptual rewrite.

## Specific actionable fixes

### Style-guide violations

- Quote: **“The harder problem sits behind that case.”**  
  Problem: punchy announcement sentence / vague metaphor.  
  Fix: replace with a direct thesis sentence, e.g. “The case raises the harder population question: how should we count harms that are visible against benefits that are mostly unobserved?”

- Quote: **“Somewhere among the people who message a chatbot at night…”**  
  Problem: mild scene-setting and unverified specificity.  
  Fix: “Some users may receive a useful crisis response, but those counterfactual cases usually leave no public record.”

- Quote: **“The ledger is lopsided in what it records, and public judgment settles on the column it can read.”**  
  Problem: ledger/column metaphor is a bit essayistic.  
  Fix: “The public record overrepresents visible harms and underrepresents prevented harms.”

- Quote: **“‘Do no harm,’ read as a guarantee that no user is ever left worse off, is not an option…”**  
  Problem: banned “not X” construction.  
  Fix: “For a population-scale intervention, the relevant safety question is how often users are helped or harmed, and which groups bear each outcome.”

- Quote: **“These are model estimates, not exact counts…”**  
  Problem: banned negation scaffolding, though the caveat is needed.  
  Fix: “These model estimates shift with age and assumptions.”

- Quote: **“They are the price paid…”**  
  Problem: rhetorical/moral flourish.  
  Fix: “USPSTF treats these harms as part of the net-benefit calculation; they are reasons to estimate the trade-off carefully.”

- Quote: **“Putting benefit and harm in the same currency forces the trade into the open…”**  
  Problem: metaphor-heavy.  
  Fix: “NNT and NNH express outcome-rate differences in comparable units, so the decision can compare magnitudes explicitly.”

- Quote: **“Childhood vaccination, water fluoridation, and speed limits rest on the same pattern…”**  
  Problem: broad unsupported flourish and a triadic list.  
  Fix: cut it, or replace with one sourced public-health example.

- Quote: **“The episode is a warning about method…”**  
  Problem: punchy setup.  
  Fix: “The antidepressant episode matters here because it shows how a policy can reduce a visible treatment harm while possibly increasing a less visible untreated harm.”

- Quote: **“The fourth difficulty is distribution.”**  
  Problem: fragment-like announcement.  
  Fix: “The distribution of harms and benefits creates a separate problem.”

- Quote: **“None of this argues for giving up on the arithmetic; it argues for the opposite…”**  
  Problem: explicitly banned “opposite” antithesis.  
  Fix: “These difficulties make the arithmetic more necessary, because uncounted trade-offs still shape decisions.”

- Quote: **“Honest accounting is easy to state and hard to carry out.”**

- Quote: **“Refusing to count does not protect anyone; it guarantees…”**

### Factual grounding

- Quote: **“treating the chatbot's outputs as a product that could carry liability rather than as protected speech.”**  
  Fix: add “at the motion-to-dismiss stage.” The May 2025 ruling allowed claims to proceed and rejected a First Amendment dismissal at that stage; it did not finally decide liability or the full speech question. Spot-check: Tech Policy Press tracker and CBS settlement coverage support the basic timeline; CBS reports confidential settlement terms and no disclosed terms.

- Quote: **“Google and Character.AI settled the case and several related suits in early 2026…”**  
  Fix: keep only if the Further reading source actually supports “several related suits”; CBS verifies the Florida family settlement, but the broader “several related suits” needs the CNBC source to remain in Further reading or the sentence should say “settled the case.”

- Quote: **“more than a million such conversations a week”**  
  Fix: OpenAI’s figure is company-reported and appears to refer to weekly users/people with conversations showing explicit indicators, not necessarily distinct conversations. Use: “more than a million weekly users have conversations with explicit indicators…”

- Quote: **“if one in a million helps someone survive a crisis, many of those happen too, as a matter of arithmetic.”**  
  Problem: implies prevented suicides are established.  
  Fix: “If the true helpful-interaction rate is even one in a million, the absolute number would be large; the problem is that this rate is not currently measured.”

- Quote: **“on the order of a thousand or more false-positive results per thousand women…”**  
  Fix: recheck the denominator and use the exact USPSTF/JAMA modeling number. The 8 deaths averted per 1,000 for biennial screening ages 40–74 is supported, but false positives differ depending on whether the count is women with at least one false-positive recall, total false-positive recalls across rounds, benign biopsies, or overdiagnoses. The current wording is too loose.

- Quote: **“That deflection is a false negative that can end in a death…”**  
  Problem: if the model detects distress and gives a hotline, this is not technically a false negative; it is an inadequate intervention or failed escalation.  
  Fix: “That deflection may reduce visible liability while failing a user who needed a more effective response.”

- Quote: **“the ED-SAFE trial worked this way…”**  
  Fix: acceptable, but be precise: ED-SAFE compared treatment as usual, universal screening, and a universal-screening-plus-intervention phase; the intervention phase reduced subsequent attempts. Do not quote a percentage unless checked against the primary paper.
