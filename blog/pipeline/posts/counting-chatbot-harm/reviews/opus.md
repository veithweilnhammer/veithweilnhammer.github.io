# Review — opus

**Thesis (one sentence):** At population scale a mental-health chatbot will both
prevent and cause serious harm, so it must be judged by an explicit net-benefit
calculation — the moral arithmetic behind screening and NNT/NNH — rather than by
its most visible tragedy.

The draft holds this one argument start to finish, covers every "must cover"
item, stands alone (no series framing/CTA), and its factual spine is sound
(spot-checks below). The dominant problem is style: the prose leans repeatedly on
the single construction the guide bans hardest — antithesis / "opposite-trade"
parallelism. That pass is the main work.

## Priority 1 — Antithesis / "opposite-trade" parallelism (the #1 hard ban, pervasive)

The guide bans "parallel 'trade' constructions used for rhythm" and mirrored
"A did X; B did the opposite" pairs. The draft uses this shape ~8 times. Each
needs de-mirroring into plain, separate statements.

1. **Mirrored sentence pair (worst instance).**
   > "A system tuned to escalate every ambiguous expression of sadness will catch
   > more genuine emergencies, but it will also frighten users and burden crisis
   > services. A system tuned to keep rapport and avoid alarm feels more useful to
   > many users while missing some whose messages carry signs of real danger."
   Fix: state the threshold trade-off once, without the "A system tuned to X … A
   system tuned to Y" mirror. e.g. "Wherever the escalation threshold is set, it
   trades one error for the other: a lower threshold catches more real
   emergencies but frightens users and floods crisis services with false alarms,
   while a higher one feels more useful day to day and misses some users in real
   danger."

2. > "A therapist who sees forty patients has a caseload small enough that 'harm
   > no one' is close to a workable standard; a service used by tens of millions
   > of people every week is not."
   The "X is workable; Y is not" mirror. Fix: "For a therapist with forty
   patients, 'harm no one' is close to a workable standard. It stops being one for
   a service used by tens of millions of people a week, where rare events are
   near-certain in both directions."

3. > "if one interaction in a million ends catastrophically, many do across the
   > user base; if one in a million helps someone survive a crisis, many of those
   > happen too"
   The "if X … if Y …" parallel. Fix: fold into one sentence — "At that volume,
   one-in-a-million events — catastrophic and life-saving alike — both happen many
   times over."

4. > "A prevented harm produces an absence."
   Short mirror of "produces a legible trail," reads as a fragment-for-effect.
   Fix: merge into the preceding sentence as a full clause: "…while a prevented
   harm produces only an absence — no case number, and often no one who knows what
   was averted."

5. Recurring adjective-pair antitheses to unwind (pick plainer phrasing):
   > "a small, often identifiable harm and a larger, mostly invisible benefit"
   > "removing a documented, treatment-caused harm may have raised an
   > undocumented, treatment-prevented one"
   > "delivering small amounts of comfort or small amounts of distortion"
   Each is the same balanced-opposites cadence; vary or flatten at least half.

## Priority 2 — Punchy setup / throat-clearing sentences (hard ban)

1. > "Honest accounting is easy to state and hard to carry out."
   This is exactly the banned "the claim is simple, though its consequences are
   not" throat-clearing. Fix: cut it and open the paragraph on the substance —
   "Honest accounting means measuring the benefit side rather than assuming it is
   zero, which requires comparison groups…"

2. > "The harder problem sits behind that case."
   Punchy announcement sentence that advertises the point instead of stating it.
   Fix: delete and let the next sentence ("A system in conversation with tens of
   millions…") carry it directly.

## Priority 3 — Piled-up lists for rhythm (soft ban)

- > "transcripts a complaint can quote, a date, a docket, a person who can be
  > named" — four-item rhythm list; cut to two ("transcripts a complaint can
  > quote, a docket with a date").
- Closing four-verb crescendo: > "count both columns, state the value judgments …
  say who bears each side, and keep the severe cases in view once the average is
  computed." Trim to the two that matter to avoid the rhythmic pile-up.
- Minor: "redesign, restrictions, and accountability" (triad) — fine, but note the
  pattern repeats.

## Priority 4 — Minor wording / softeners

- > "This asymmetry would matter less if these systems were rare." Mild
  understatement opener; prefer the positive: "The asymmetry only bites because
  these systems are not rare."
- > "the alternative to counting is not neutrality" — negation-flavored opener;
  say it positively: "refusing to count is itself a choice, made by whichever harm
  is visible."

## Factual grounding (verified — no errors found)

- **USPSTF/breast screening:** "roughly eight breast-cancer deaths per thousand"
  and "a thousand or more false-positive results per thousand" match
  Trentham-Dietz et al., *JAMA* 2024 (8.2 deaths averted; 1,376 false positives
  per 1,000, ages 40–74). Correctly flagged as model estimates. ✔
- **OpenAI scale:** 0.15% of weekly users / >1M conversations/week is accurate and
  correctly framed as company-reported scale indicator, not epidemiology. ✔
- **Garcia/Character.AI:** Setzer (14, FL, Feb 2024), Oct 2024 suit, May 2025
  ruling (Judge Conway — product not protected speech), Jan 2026 confidential
  settlement with no admission — all correct; causation correctly presented as
  contested and unadjudicated. ✔
- **ED-SAFE (Miller 2017):** correctly described as universal ED screening + brief
  intervention reducing later suicide attempts; the draft wisely avoids quoting a
  specific effect size (the 30%/26.3%→18.3% figure exists but secondary-source
  variability was flagged). ✔ Optional: note it was a sequential three-phase
  design, not a randomized parallel trial, so "comparing … against those who
  received usual care" slightly over-tidies the method.
- **Adolescent SSRIs / boxed warning:** correlational reading correctly hedged
  ("remains contested"). ✔

## Structure / brief / standalone

- Leads with the concrete anchor, states the claim in the first paragraph,
  develops it in clear numbered difficulties, closes on the substantive point. A
  non-specialist can follow it. ✔
- Watch the do-NOT-re-tell boundary: para 8 ("the benefit side is largely
  unmeasured") and para 14 ("measuring the benefit side rather than assuming it is
  zero") edge toward the "we aren't measuring at all" argument that post 10 owns.
  It stays on the "how to count once we do" side, but keep the emphasis on method,
  not on the absence of measurement per se.
- Length ~2,070 words (draft only) — slightly over the 2,000 target; the Priority
  1–3 cuts will bring it in range.

## Top 3 priorities

1. Do a de-antithesis pass: rewrite the ~8 mirrored "opposite-trade" pairs
   (esp. the "A system tuned to…/A system tuned to…" pair) into plain, separate
   statements.
2. Cut the two setup/throat-clearing sentences ("Honest accounting is easy to
   state and hard to carry out." / "The harder problem sits behind that case.").
3. Trim the rhythm lists (the "transcripts…date…docket…person" four-item list and
   the closing four-verb crescendo).

**Verdict:** minor fixes — factually sound and structurally complete; needs one
focused style pass to strip the banned antithesis/setup constructions.
