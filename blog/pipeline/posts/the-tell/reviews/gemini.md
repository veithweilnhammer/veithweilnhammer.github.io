# Review: the-tell (gemini)

## 1. Style-guide violations

The draft is very strong, but it contains several of the banned "not X, it is Y" (or "rather than") constructions, evocative image lists, and punchy setup sentences. 

**"Not X, it is Y" / "Rather than" constructions:**
* Offending text: "...and for them the words are genuinely hard to locate rather than deliberately withheld."
  * Fix: "...and for them the words are genuinely hard to locate."
* Offending text: "Stigma changes the answer to 'how are you,' not the rhythm with which the answer is typed."
  * Fix: "Stigma changes the spoken answer without altering the rhythm of the typing."
* Offending text: "...inherited responses you can observe rather than a coating laid over a private feeling."
  * Fix: "...inherited responses you can observe."
* Offending text: "...arrives at a scale and in a setting a clinic cannot match, in the middle of a person's real day rather than in a fifteen-minute appointment they first have to recognize they need."
  * Fix: "...arrives at a scale and in a setting a clinic cannot match, tracking people continuously during their daily routines."
* Offending text: "...timing and error patterns rather than the content of any message..."
  * Fix: "...metadata of smartphone typing, such as timing and error patterns..."
* Offending text: "...because a tell is leaked rather than offered."
  * Fix: "...because a tell is leaked unintentionally."

**Evocative image-lists / fragment enumeration:**
* Offending text: "How long someone pauses before starting to type, how fast and how evenly the keys fall, how often a sentence is deleted and rewritten, how a cursor accelerates and overshoots and corrects, how a thumb scrolls and stops, and how long the hesitation runs before a message is finally sent are all generated as a by-product of normal use."
  * Fix: "Everyday interaction metrics—like typing speed, correction frequency, and cursor acceleration—are generated as a continuous by-product of normal device use."

**Punchy setup sentences:**
* Offending text: "That gap between what a person controls and what escapes anyway is the whole point of a tell."
  * Fix: Combine with the next sentence: "A tell emerges from the gap between what a person controls and what escapes anyway: a poker player chooses every word..."
* Offending text: "The mechanism behind this is well understood."
  * Fix: "The mechanism behind this is grounded in motor-control theory: every deliberate movement is planned..."
* Offending text: "None of this turns behavior into a clear window."
  * Fix: "Behavioral signals remain noisy and ambiguous: a slow, error-filled bout of typing..."

**Rhetorical crescendos:**
* Offending text: "...notice distress that a person cannot yet see, cannot name, or has decided not to speak."
  * Fix: "...notice distress in patients who cannot or will not report it."

## 2. Factual grounding
The draft perfectly adheres to the provided evidence block. The studies (Slepian, Clement, Sifneos, BiAffect) and Darwin are cited accurately. No overclaims are made; the limits of digital phenotyping (noisiness, group-average accuracy, lack of mouse-signal reliability) are well-stated.

## 3. Structure & clarity
The logic is clear and intuitive. The transition from the poker tell (the analogy) to self-report failures (the clinical problem) to interaction tracking (the solution) to consent/noise (the limits) flows seamlessly for a non-specialist reader. 

## 4. One self-contained argument
**Thesis:** The way people interact with devices involuntarily leaks mental state information that self-report misses because of stigma, alexithymia, and lack of insight. 
The post excellently develops this single argument from start to finish without branching into unrelated topics. 

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
The post hits every requirement in the brief (poker, self-report issues, digital phenotyping, Darwin, limits, importance). It stands completely alone, avoiding any series framing or calls to action. The light integration of MAILA is handled perfectly without over-explaining the study.

## 6. Top 3 priorities
1. Strip out the "not X, it is Y" and "rather than" negation/antithesis constructions in paragraphs 3, 4, 6, 7, and 8.
2. Remove the evocative image list of interaction traces in paragraph 7 and the concluding triadic phrase.
3. Eliminate the isolated punchy setup sentences (e.g., "The mechanism behind this is well understood") by integrating them into informative declarative sentences.

## Verdict
ship-with-minor-edits: The draft perfectly tracks the brief and maintains a single coherent argument, requiring only surgical style edits to remove negation constructions and evocative lists.
