# Stage 3 Review: Gemini

## 1. Style-guide violations

- **Antithesis / negation-definition ("not X, it is Y"):**
  - *Draft:* "Most of those messages were not work. More than seventy percent were ordinary life..."
    *Rewrite:* "More than seventy percent of those messages were ordinary life..."
  - *Draft:* "Missing controls are not a bookkeeping problem that can be patched later. They change what is knowable at all."
    *Rewrite:* "Missing controls permanently change what is knowable."
  - *Draft:* "...not as a wave of sudden collapses." (End of paragraph about lead/tobacco showing up as slow shifts)
    *Rewrite:* Drop the clause. Just say: "Lead and tobacco showed up as slow shifts in population averages that took decades to separate from background noise."
  - *Draft:* "Whether we do is not a technical question. It is a choice about whether we would rather know."
    *Rewrite:* "Whether we do is a choice about whether we would rather know."

- **Punchy setup / announcement sentences:**
  - *Draft:* "Calling it an intervention is meant literally."
    *Rewrite:* "Conversational AI functions as an intervention because a system that a person consults many times a week sits in the path of several processes that psychology has studied for a century."
  - *Draft:* "That is the part worth taking seriously."
    *Rewrite:* Delete entirely. Start the paragraph with "The exposure is already global."

- **Rhetorical repetition / AI cadence:**
  - *Draft:* "It shapes mood, because... It shapes belief, because... It shapes attention, because... And it shapes social life, because..."
    *Rewrite:* This four-part repetition is classic AI rhetoric and violates the rule against parallel constructions used for rhythm. Combine them plainly: "The tools shape mood, belief, attention, and social life. People bring distress to them, use them for factual and moral questions, send them half-formed thoughts, and substitute them for human conversation."

- **Pseudo-poetic metaphors:**
  - *Draft:* "The window in which a clean measurement is cheap opens early and closes quietly."
    *Rewrite:* "The window for cheap, clean measurement passes as adoption nears universal."

- **Rhetorical understatement opener:**
  - *Draft:* "Running an exposure for years before measuring it is not new, and its history is not reassuring."
    *Rewrite:* "We have a history of running exposures for years before measuring them."

## 2. Factual grounding
The draft is extremely well-grounded. The Axios, TechCrunch, NBER, Anthropic, and MIT/OpenAI figures are integrated cleanly. The historical precedents (Patterson/Needleman for lead, Doll & Hill for tobacco, Haidt vs Odgers for social media) match the provided evidence exactly. No overclaims detected; the draft accurately frames the absence of measurement as applying equally to harm and benefit. 

## 3. Structure & clarity
The structure perfectly tracks the brief. It leads immediately with the claim about population scale and lack of baselines. The logical flow from the current situation to the explanation of why missing controls matter, to historical precedents, and finally to epidemiological solutions is clear. A non-specialist could follow this start to finish.

## 4. One self-contained argument
**Thesis:** We deployed conversational AI as a population-scale psychological intervention without baselines or controls, making it nearly impossible to definitively measure its harms or benefits.

The post develops this single argument relentlessly. Every paragraph, including the historical anchors, serves this thesis. It does not wander into predicting specific harms or re-hashing side arguments. 

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
The post hits every required "must cover" point from the brief, including the honest caveat about benefits. It reads as a completely standalone essay. There are no series framings, "next time" teasers, or calls to subscribe.

## 6. Top 3 priorities
1. **Remove antithesis and negative framing:** Fix the "not X, it is Y" constructions (e.g., "not a bookkeeping problem", "not a technical question", "not work").
2. **Flatten the rhythmic anaphora:** Rewrite the "It shapes [x], because..." list. The repetition sounds highly rhetorical and violates the cadence rule.
3. **Cut poetic flourishes and empty setups:** Remove lines like "opens early and closes quietly" and "That is the part worth taking seriously."

`ship-with-minor-edits` - The draft flawlessly executes the structural brief, stays strictly on its single thesis, and integrates the factual evidence perfectly, requiring only line edits to strip out a few rhetorical flourishes and antithesis constructions that violate the style guide.
