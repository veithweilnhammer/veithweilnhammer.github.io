# Stage 3 Review: Gemini

## 1. Style-guide violations
The draft relies heavily on banned "not X, it is Y" antithesis constructions, punchy setup/announcement sentences, and occasional evocative scene-setting.

**Antithesis / "not X, it is Y" constructions:**
* ✗ "its breach reads as a wrong done to the person rather than a lapse in their willpower."
  *Rewrite:* "its breach constitutes a recognized wrong done to the person."
* ✗ "a right places the duty on the party that would intrude, not on the party who must stay perpetually vigilant."
  *Rewrite:* "a right places the duty to avoid intrusion directly on the system's builders."
* ✗ "instead of leaving individuals to cope alone against tools designed to overwhelm coping."
  *Rewrite:* "Structural remedies—such as constraints on defaults and limits on persuasive features—replace the expectation that individuals must individually resist tools designed to overwhelm them."
* ✗ "Data privacy governs the record of what a person did; it does not challenge the legitimacy of the environment that shaped the doing."
  *Rewrite:* "Data privacy governs the record of what a person did, leaving the legitimacy of the environment that shaped their actions unexamined."
* ✗ "The regulated conduct is that adaptive, testing-driven exploitation, not absorption as such."
  *Rewrite:* "Regulation must target this adaptive, testing-driven exploitation while leaving ordinary absorption unaffected."
* ✗ "regulate design conditions ... rather than viewpoints."
  *Rewrite:* "A workable right must regulate design conditions ... leaving viewpoint expression alone."
* ✗ "puts the responsibility where the asymmetry actually lies — with the people who design for capture, and not with the person sitting alone at night with a device built to keep them there."
  *Rewrite:* "Naming that interest as a right assigns responsibility directly to the designers of these capture mechanisms."

**Punchy setup/announcement sentences:**
* ✗ "Many widely used systems are built to do this, and they are built well."
  *Rewrite:* Cut entirely. Start directly with: "Social feeds, mobile games, and conversational AI are optimized against measured engagement..."
* ✗ "The usual advice for this treats it as a private discipline problem."
  *Rewrite:* "Standard advice treats this engagement as a private discipline problem: set screen-time limits..."
* ✗ "Other safety problems went through the same shift in description, and the history is instructive."
  *Rewrite:* "Automotive and workplace safety histories show a similar shift from blaming individual behavior to regulating structural design."
* ✗ "Framing the interest as a right does three things the discipline framing cannot."
  *Rewrite:* Cut this throat-clearing. Just start the paragraph: "Treating the interest as a right names the protected interest plainly, shifts responsibility to the system builders, and licenses structural remedies."
* ✗ "The framing has real limits, and they should be stated plainly. The first is definitional."
  *Rewrite:* "A workable right must distinguish between ordinary engaging design and asymmetric, testing-driven exploitation."

**Pseudo-poetic scene-setting and crescendos:**
* ✗ "The intrusion is easiest to see at its sharpest edge: a lonely teenager messaged late at night by a system built to feel like a confidant, or a grieving adult offered an endlessly available companion that never tires of them."
  *Rewrite:* "The intrusion is clearest when a system acts as a confidant to a vulnerable user, capturing their attention and simulating companionship during moments of distress."

## 2. Factual grounding
* ✗ "Whatever a jury eventually decides..."
  *Finding:* A quick web search verifies that the *Garcia v. Character Technologies* lawsuit reached a confidential settlement in January 2026. A jury will not decide it.
  *Fix:* Update the text to reflect the January 2026 settlement while noting that the legal question it raised remains central.

## 3. Structure & clarity
The draft effectively sets up the problem using a concrete case (Sewell Setzer III). The progression from the failure of willpower, to historical analogies, to the rights framing is logical and accessible to a non-specialist. However, the core thesis ("A more accurate framing treats protection from these systems as a right") doesn't arrive until paragraph 5. The first four paragraphs are entirely setup. Consider moving the thesis up slightly to anchor the reader's understanding earlier.

## 4. One self-contained argument
The post's single thesis is: Protection from systems optimizing for attention and intimacy must be framed as a right to an undisturbed inner life, rather than a personal discipline problem. The post rigorously develops this single argument from start to finish without branching into unrelated topics.

## 5. Brief compliance & standalone
The draft fully complies with the brief. It covers the failure of the willpower framing, the value of a rights framework, the distinction from data privacy, and the relevant precedents and limits. It successfully avoids re-telling the history of the attention economy or behavioral leakage. The post stands alone perfectly with no series framing, next/previous links, or subscribe CTAs.

## 6. Top 3 priorities
1. **Remove antithesis ("not X, it is Y") and parallel "trade" constructions.** Convert the heavy reliance on negative contrasts (e.g., "rather than a lapse", "not on the party", "not absorption as such") into positive, direct claims.
2. **Cut punchy announcement sentences.** Eliminate short, dramatic setups at the beginning of paragraphs ("The framing has real limits, and they should be stated plainly. The first is definitional.") and integrate their points directly into the prose.
3. **Update the Character.AI factual status.** Correct the assumption that the case is heading to a jury ("Whatever a jury eventually decides"), as it was settled in January 2026.
