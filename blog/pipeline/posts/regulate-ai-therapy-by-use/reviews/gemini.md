# Review: Gemini

## 1. Style-guide violations

- **Antithesis / negation-definition ("not X, but Y"):**
  - *Quote:* "Harm and benefit in this setting follow the conversation, not the marketing page."
  - *Fix:* "Harm and benefit in this setting arise from what the system says during the conversation."

- **Punchy setup sentences:**
  - *Quote:* "That is the case for a use-based trigger." (Start of paragraph 6)
  - *Fix:* Delete this dramatic opening and merge with the next sentence: "When a system is functioning as a mental-health product for a given user—when the exchange is a sustained one..."

- **Rhetorical understatement opener:**
  - *Quote:* "The objections to this are real, and the strongest is enforcement." (Start of paragraph 7)
  - *Fix:* "Enforcement is the primary challenge for a use-based standard."

- **Parallel "trade" constructions for rhythm:**
  - *Quote:* "A rule aimed at the label is easy to draft and easy to route around... A rule aimed at the function is harder to write and harder to enforce..."
  - *Fix:* "Regulating by label is easy to draft but easily bypassed by changing marketing copy. Regulating by function requires bounding rules carefully so they don't overreach, but it actually reaches the conversations where harm or benefit occur."

- **Throat-clearing / Abstract intro:**
  - *Quote:* "None of this makes a chatbot a good therapist, and none of it settles whether AI belongs in mental-health care at all. The narrower point is about where a law points."
  - *Fix:* State the point directly. "Whether AI belongs in mental-health care is debated, but the immediate regulatory question is coverage."

- **Rhetorical crescendo closing:**
  - *Quote:* "When more than a million people a week are taking their worst moments to a system that no therapy-specific law touches, the marketing label is the least informative thing about what that system has become."
  - *Fix:* "With more than a million people a week discussing mental-health crises with general-purpose tools, the product label no longer describes how the software is used."
